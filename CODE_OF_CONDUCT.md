# Code of Conduct

Polaris is a small public project, and I want the issue tracker and discussions to stay useful for people who are trying to install, test, debug, or improve it.

## Expected Behavior

- Be patient with people who are new to the project, Linux game streaming, or open source.
- Assume good intent when something is unclear, then ask for clarification.
- Keep bug reports and reviews focused on the behavior, logs, code, and user impact.
- Respect that maintainers and contributors may have limited time.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, personal attacks, slurs, or threats.
- Repeated off-topic arguments after a maintainer asks to move on.
- Posting private information, credentials, exploit details, or logs that include someone else's secrets.
- Demanding unpaid support or release timelines.

## Reporting

For normal moderation concerns, use the relevant issue or discussion thread and keep the report factual. For security-sensitive problems, follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) instead of posting details publicly.

I may edit, hide, lock, or remove comments that make the project harder to use or maintain.
